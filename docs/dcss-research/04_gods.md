# DCSS Gods Guide

This guide provides comprehensive information on all deities in Dungeon Crawl Stone Soup (DCSS), including piety mechanics, abilities, and conduct rules.

## Overview

Gods are powerful entities that grant unique abilities and gifts to their followers in exchange for worship and piety. Choosing the right god is crucial for most character builds.

---

## Good Gods

Good gods require strict adherence to moral conduct and generally forbid evil acts like necromancy or using unholy items.

### Zin
**Philosophy**: Law, purity, and discipline.
- **Piety Gain**: Tithing gold (10%), killing unclean/chaotic creatures.
- **Abilities**:
  - **Recite**: Debuff unclean/chaotic monsters.
  - **Vitalisation**: Immunity to poison/confusion, stat boost.
  - **Imprison**: Trap an enemy in a silver cage.
  - **Sanctuary**: Create a protected area that pushes enemies away.
- **Conduct**: No evil magic, no mutations, no attacking neutrals.

### The Shining One (TSO)
**Philosophy**: Honorable combat against evil.
- **Piety Gain**: Killing undead, demons, and evil creatures.
- **Abilities**:
  - **Halo**: Reveal invisible enemies, increase accuracy.
  - **Cleansing Flame**: Holy damage to nearby evil creatures.
  - **Summon Divine Warrior**: Call an Angel or Daeva to fight.
  - **Holy Brand**: Permanently brand a weapon with Holy Wrath.
- **Conduct**: No stabbing, no invisibility, no evil magic/items.

### Elyvilon
**Philosophy**: Healing and pacifism.
- **Piety Gain**: Exploration, pacifying monsters.
- **Abilities**:
  - **Purification**: Cure status effects and restore stats.
  - **Heal Other**: Heal a monster (may pacify it).
  - **Heal Self**: Powerful self-healing.
  - **Divine Vigour**: Temporary boost to max HP and MP.
- **Conduct**: No necromancy, no attacking holy beings.

## Neutral Gods

Neutral gods have various philosophies and generally allow more flexibility in conduct than good gods.

### Okawaru
**Philosophy**: The Warmaster, god of battle and self-reliance.
- **Piety Gain**: Killing powerful monsters.
- **Abilities**:
  - **Heroism**: +5 to all combat skills.
  - **Finesse**: Double attack speed.
  - **Duel**: Challenge a single enemy to a 1v1 arena fight.
- **Gifts**: Weapons and armor.
- **Conduct**: No allies (Okawaru wants you to fight alone).

### Vehumet
**Philosophy**: Destructive magic.
- **Piety Gain**: Killing monsters with spells.
- **Abilities**:
  - **MP from Kills**: Gain magic points when enemies die.
  - **Spell Success Boost**: Increased success rate for destructive spells.
  - **Range Boost**: Increased range for destructive spells.
- **Gifts**: Destructive spells.

### Sif Muna
**Philosophy**: Knowledge and mastery of all magic.
- **Piety Gain**: Training magic skills, killing monsters.
- **Abilities**:
  - **Channel Magic**: Restore MP.
  - **Forget Spell**: Remove a learned spell.
  - **Divine Exegesis**: Cast any spell from any book you carry.
- **Gifts**: Spellbooks.

### Trog
**Philosophy**: Rage and hatred of magic.
- **Piety Gain**: Killing monsters (especially spellcasters).
- **Abilities**:
  - **Berserk**: Enter a powerful rage.
  - **Trog's Hand**: Regeneration and high magic resistance.
  - **Broothers in Arms**: Summon a powerful berserk ally.
- **Gifts**: Weapons (usually axes or maces).
- **Conduct**: No spellcasting, no training magic skills.

### Cheibriados
**Philosophy**: Slowness and deliberation.
- **Piety Gain**: Killing fast creatures.
- **Abilities**:
  - **Stat Boosts**: Massive increases to all stats (Str, Int, Dex).
  - **Bend Time**: Slow nearby enemies.
  - **Slouch**: Damage all nearby enemies based on their speed.
  - **Step From Time**: Escape to a safe moment in time.
- **Conduct**: No hasting, no fast movement.

### Ashenzari
**Philosophy**: Curses and forbidden knowledge.
- **Piety Gain**: Cursing your own equipment.
- **Abilities**:
  - **Skill Boosts**: Increased skill levels based on cursed slots.
  - **See Invisible**: Innate see invisible.
  - **Clarity**: Immunity to confusion.
  - **Monster/Map Detection**: Reveal nearby enemies and map layout.
- **Conduct**: No uncursing items (without piety loss).

### Gozag
**Philosophy**: Greed and commerce.
- **Piety Gain**: N/A (Gozag uses gold instead of piety).
- **Abilities**:
  - **Potion Petition**: Pay gold for powerful potion effects.
  - **Call Merchant**: Pay gold to summon a shop.
  - **Bribe Branch**: Pay gold to make monsters in a branch neutral.
- **Special**: Enemies drop gold instead of corpses.

### Ru
**Philosophy**: Sacrifice and enlightenment.
- **Piety Gain**: Making permanent sacrifices (e.g., losing an eye, a hand, or HP).
- **Abilities**:
  - **Aura of Power**: Redirect damage and debuff enemies.
  - **Draw Out Power**: Heal and restore MP.
  - **Power Leap**: Jump and cause an explosion.
  - **Apocalypse**: Massive damage and debuffs to all in LOS.

### Hepliaklqana
**Philosophy**: Ancestry and remembrance.
- **Piety Gain**: Exploration.
- **Abilities**:
  - **Ancestor Ally**: A permanent spectral ally (Knight, Battlemage, or Hexer).
  - **Transference**: Swap places with your ancestor.
  - **Idealise**: Powerful buff for your ancestor.

### Wu Jian
**Philosophy**: Martial arts and movement.
- **Piety Gain**: Killing monsters.
- **Abilities**:
  - **Lunge**: Attack while moving towards an enemy.
  - **Whirlwind**: Attack while moving around an enemy.
  - **Wall Jump**: Jump off a wall to attack.
  - **Heavenly Storm**: Create a cloud of fog that buffs your attacks.

## Evil & Chaotic Gods

Evil gods often demand blood or souls, while chaotic gods are unpredictable.

### Makhleb
**Philosophy**: Destruction and chaos.
- **Piety Gain**: Killing monsters.
- **Abilities**:
  - **HP from Kills**: Heal when enemies die.
  - **Unleash Destruction**: Fire random destructive bolts.
  - **Summon Greater Demon**: Call powerful demons to fight.

### Kikubaaqudgha
**Philosophy**: Necromancy and death.
- **Piety Gain**: Killing monsters.
- **Abilities**:
  - **Unearth Wretches**: Summon corpses/skeletons.
  - **Torment Protection**: Reduced damage from torment.
  - **Receive Spells**: Gain necromancy spells.
  - **Pain Brand**: Brand a weapon with Pain.

### Yredelemnul
**Philosophy**: Death and the undead.
- **Piety Gain**: Killing monsters while your black torch is lit.
- **Abilities**:
  - **Animate Dead**: Raise fallen enemies as zombies.
  - **Hurl Torchlight**: Fire explosion.
  - **Bind Soul**: Create a permanent undead ally from a powerful foe.

### Beogh
**Philosophy**: The Orcish god, god of outcasts.
- **Piety Gain**: Killing hostile priests and monsters.
- **Abilities**:
  - **Convert Orcs**: Turn hostile orcs into loyal followers.
  - **Smiting**: Direct damage to an enemy.
  - **Blood for Blood**: Summon orcish reinforcements.
- **Restriction**: Only Hill Orcs can worship Beogh initially.

### Lugonu
**Philosophy**: The Abyss and corruption.
- **Piety Gain**: Killing monsters, banishing enemies.
- **Abilities**:
  - **Banish**: Send an enemy to the Abyss.
  - **Corrupt**: Corrupt the current level, spawning abyss monsters.
  - **Enter/Exit Abyss**: Safe travel to and from the Abyss.
- **Gifts**: Distortion brand.

### Jiyva
**Philosophy**: Slime and mutation.
- **Piety Gain**: Allowing slimes to consume items.
- **Abilities**:
  - **Regeneration**: Massive boost to HP and MP regen.
  - **Slimify**: Turn an enemy into a neutral slime.
  - **Mutations**: Gain helpful slime mutations.
- **Special**: Slimes are neutral and will eat items on the floor.

### Xom
**Philosophy**: Chaos and entertainment.
- **Piety Gain**: N/A (Xom's mood changes randomly).
- **Abilities**: N/A (Xom acts randomly, granting boons or causing disasters based on his mood).
- **Special**: Xom is entertained by "interesting" events (combat, danger, weird occurrences).

## Piety Progression

Most gods use a 6-star piety system:
- **0 Stars**: New follower.
- **1-2 Stars**: Basic abilities unlocked.
- **3-4 Stars**: Advanced abilities and gifts begin.
- **5 Stars**: Most powerful abilities unlocked.
- **6 Stars**: Maximum favor, ultimate rewards.

---

## Obsolete Gods

- **Pakellas**: God of devices and evocations. Removed in 0.19.
- **Nemelex Xobeh**: God of cards and decks. (Still in game, but mechanics have changed significantly over versions).
